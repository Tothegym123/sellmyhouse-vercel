Cash Offer vs. Realtor: Which Is Better in Hampton Roads?

Published March 17, 2026 · Updated April 10, 2026

When it's time to sell your home in Hampton Roads, you face a fundamental choice: should you accept a cash offer or list with a realtor? Both paths have genuine advantages depending on your situation, timeline, and priorities. This guide breaks down the real numbers so you can make an informed decision.

Tidewater Property Rentals LLC provides honest cash offers for homes across Virginia Beach, Chesapeake, Norfolk, Hampton, Newport News, and Portsmouth. Here's the full comparison.

The Traditional Realtor Route

Listing with a real estate agent is the most common way to sell a home. An agent lists your property on the MLS, markets it to buyers, handles negotiations, and guides you through closing. In exchange, you pay a commission — typically 5-6% of the sale price in Hampton Roads.

For a $300,000 home, that's $15,000-$18,000 in commissions alone. Add in closing costs (1-3%), potential repair credits, staging, photography, and 3-6 months of carrying costs, and the total expense of selling with an agent can reach $25,000-$40,000.

The Cash Offer Route

A cash home buyer like Tidewater Property Rentals makes you a direct offer based on the property's value and condition. There are no commissions, no closing costs (we pay them), no repairs, and no waiting. The trade-off is that a cash offer is typically below full market value — usually 70-85% of a home's after-repair value.

For the same $300,000 home, a cash offer might range from $210,000 to $255,000. But with zero commissions, zero repair costs, zero closing costs, and closing in 7-14 days instead of 3-6 months, the gap narrows significantly when you compare actual net proceeds.

Side-By-Side Cost Comparison

Let's run the real numbers on a $300,000 Hampton Roads home that needs $15,000 in repairs:

Selling With a Realtor

  • Sale price: $300,000
  • Agent commission (6%): -$18,000
  • Closing costs (2%): -$6,000
  • Repairs/concessions: -$15,000
  • Carrying costs (4 months): -$8,000
  • Net proceeds: $253,000
  • Timeline: 3-6 months

Selling for Cash

  • Cash offer (80% ARV): $240,000
  • Agent commission: $0
  • Closing costs: $0 (buyer pays)
  • Repairs: $0 (sold as-is)
  • Carrying costs: $0 (close in 7-14 days)
  • Net proceeds: $240,000
  • Timeline: 7-14 days

In this example, the difference in net proceeds is just $13,000 — but you get your money 3-5 months sooner with zero hassle. For homeowners dealing with financial pressure, relocation deadlines, or properties needing major work, the cash route often makes more financial sense.

When a Realtor Makes More Sense

Listing with an agent is typically better when your home is in excellent condition with no major repairs needed, you're not in a rush and can wait 3-6 months for the right buyer, the Hampton Roads market is hot and you expect multiple offers, you want to maximize every dollar and don't mind the process, and you have the budget to stage, photograph, and maintain the home during showings.

When a Cash Offer Makes More Sense

A cash offer is typically better when you need to sell quickly (job relocation, divorce, financial hardship), the property needs major repairs you can't afford, you're dealing with an inherited or probate property, the house has code violations or is condemned, you want to avoid showings, negotiations, and uncertainty, you're facing foreclosure and need a fast closing, or you're a landlord with tenants in place who complicate traditional showings.

Red Flags to Watch For With Cash Buyers

Not all cash buyers are the same. Watch out for companies that pressure you for an immediate decision, offers that seem too low without clear justification, buyers who won't provide proof of funds, contracts with hidden fees or assignment clauses, and companies with no local presence or reviews. A legitimate cash buyer like Tidewater Property Rentals will give you time to review the offer, explain exactly how we calculated it, and never charge you any fees.

The Bottom Line

There's no universally "better" option — it depends entirely on your situation. If you have time and a move-in-ready home, a realtor can maximize your sale price. Frequently Asked Questions
