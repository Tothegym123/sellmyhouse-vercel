Fair Market Value Tips

Published July 29, 2025 · Updated February 10, 2026

Understanding Fair Market Value in Hampton Roads

Whether you are selling your home, refinancing your mortgage, or settling an estate, understanding the fair market value of your property is essential. Fair market value represents the price a willing buyer would pay a willing seller when both parties have reasonable knowledge of the relevant facts and neither is under pressure to complete the transaction.

In the Hampton Roads real estate market, property values can vary significantly between neighborhoods and even between blocks. Knowing how to accurately assess your home's value puts you in a stronger position regardless of how you choose to sell.

How Fair Market Value Is Determined

Comparable Sales Analysis

The most common method for determining fair market value is a comparative market analysis, or CMA. This involves looking at recent sales of similar properties in your area, typically within the last three to six months. Key factors include square footage, number of bedrooms and bathrooms, lot size, age of the home, and overall condition.

In Hampton Roads, location plays a particularly important role. A home near the oceanfront in Virginia Beach will command a very different price than a similar home in the western part of Chesapeake, even if the physical characteristics are comparable.

Professional Appraisal

A licensed appraiser provides an independent assessment of your home's value based on a physical inspection, comparable sales data, and their professional judgment. Appraisals typically cost between 300 and 500 dollars and are required by most mortgage lenders during a traditional sale.

Online Valuation Tools

Websites like Zillow, Redfin, and Realtor.com provide automated home value estimates. While these tools can give you a general starting point, they often lack accuracy because they cannot account for your home's specific condition, upgrades, or unique features. In the Hampton Roads market, automated estimates can be off by 10 percent or more.

Factors That Affect Your Home's Value

Several key factors influence property values in Hampton Roads. The condition of your home is one of the most significant factors. Updated kitchens, bathrooms, roofing, and HVAC systems can add considerable value, while deferred maintenance and outdated finishes may reduce it.

Proximity to military bases, schools, shopping, and waterfront access also impacts value. Flood zone status is another important consideration in Hampton Roads, as properties in high-risk flood zones may have lower values due to mandatory flood insurance requirements.

Getting the Best Value When Selling

If you plan to list your home on the traditional market, investing in minor updates and staging can help maximize your sale price. However, if your home needs significant work or you need to sell quickly, a cash offer based on current as-is condition may be the most practical approach. Cash buyers evaluate fair market value differently, factoring in repair costs and their own risk, but they offer certainty and speed in return.
