How Much Will You Get for Your Norfolk House?

Cash offers in Norfolk typically range from 70% to 85% of a home's after-repair value (ARV), depending on the condition and location. The formula we use is simple:

Cash Offer = ARV − Repair Costs − Holding Costs − Profit Margin

While that's less than what you might clear listing the house, you need to compare the net proceeds, not the gross sale price. Traditional listings come with:

  • 6% agent commissions ($18,000 on a $300,000 home)
  • 2-3% closing costs ($6,000-$9,000)
  • Repair credits and concessions (average $7,500)
  • Monthly carrying costs while listed (mortgage, taxes, insurance, utilities — often $2,500+/mo)

When you add it all up, a cash offer is often within 5-10% of what you'd actually net from a traditional sale — but you get the money 4-5 months sooner. Read our detailed breakdown of what cash buyers pay for a full analysis.
