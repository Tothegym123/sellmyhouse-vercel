Sell Inherited Property

How to Sell an Inherited Property in Hampton Roads

Inheriting a property can be both a blessing and a burden. While the home may hold sentimental value, many heirs find themselves responsible for mortgage payments, property taxes, insurance, and maintenance on a home they may not want or be able to keep. If you have inherited a home in Hampton Roads, understanding the process of selling can help you make the best decision for your situation.

Understanding Probate in Virginia

Before you can sell an inherited property in Virginia, the estate typically needs to go through probate. Probate is the legal process of validating the deceased person's will and transferring ownership of their assets. In Virginia, probate is handled through the Circuit Court in the jurisdiction where the deceased person lived.

If the property was held in a trust or had a transfer-on-death deed, you may be able to avoid probate entirely. Otherwise, the executor or administrator of the estate will need court authorization before selling the property. This process can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months depending on the complexity of the estate.

Tax Considerations for Inherited Property

One important advantage of inheriting property is the stepped-up cost basis. This means the property's tax basis is adjusted to its fair market value at the time of the previous owner's death, rather than what they originally paid for it. If you sell the property for close to its current market value, you may owe little or no capital gains tax.

However, if you hold the property for an extended period and it appreciates in value, you could be responsible for capital gains tax on the difference between the stepped-up basis and the eventual sale price. Selling sooner rather than later can help minimize this tax exposure.

Challenges of Selling an Inherited Home

Inherited properties in Hampton Roads often present unique challenges. The home may have deferred maintenance or outdated systems that need attention. There may be personal belongings that need to be sorted and removed. Multiple heirs may have different opinions about what to do with the property, making decisions more complicated.

Additionally, if you live out of state, managing the sale of a property remotely adds logistical difficulties. Coordinating repairs, showings, and inspections from a distance can be time-consuming and expensive.

Why a Cash Sale May Be Your Best Option

For many heirs, selling an inherited property to a cash buyer offers the simplest path forward. Cash buyers purchase homes in as-is condition, meaning you do not need to invest in repairs or cleanouts. The sale can close quickly, often within two to three weeks, which reduces your ongoing carrying costs for the property.

A cash sale also eliminates the uncertainty of traditional buyers whose financing may fall through. When multiple heirs are involved, a quick and certain sale helps everyone move forward and receive their share of the proceeds without prolonged disagreements.
