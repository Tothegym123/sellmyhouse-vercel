Selling a House During Divorce in Virginia

Published September 9, 2025 · Updated January 22, 2026

Navigating a Home Sale During Divorce in Virginia

Going through a divorce is one of the most stressful life events anyone can face, and figuring out what to do with the family home adds another layer of complexity. In Virginia, marital property is subject to equitable distribution, which means the court divides assets fairly but not necessarily equally.

For many couples in Hampton Roads, the family home is the largest shared asset. Understanding your options can help you make the best decision during a difficult time.

Your Options for the Marital Home

When divorcing in Virginia, you generally have three options for dealing with the family home. The first option is for one spouse to buy out the other's share of the equity. This requires refinancing the mortgage in one person's name and paying the other spouse their portion of the home's value.

The second option is to sell the home on the open market and split the proceeds. This is often the cleanest solution, though it takes time and requires both parties to agree on listing price, repairs, and agent selection.

The third option is to sell to a cash buyer for a fast, clean transaction. This approach eliminates the complications of listing, showing, and negotiating with traditional buyers. Cash sales can close in as little as two weeks, allowing both parties to move forward quickly.

Virginia Property Division Laws

Virginia follows equitable distribution principles, meaning the court considers multiple factors when dividing property. These include the length of the marriage, each spouse's contributions to the household, and the circumstances leading to the divorce. Property acquired during the marriage is generally considered marital property, while assets owned before the marriage may be classified as separate property.

If you and your spouse cannot agree on how to handle the home, the court may order a sale. Having a cash offer ready can expedite this process and reduce conflict between the parties.

Why Speed Matters in Divorce Home Sales

Extended home sale timelines during divorce can lead to increased costs and emotional strain. Both parties may still be responsible for mortgage payments, property taxes, insurance, and maintenance while the home sits on the market. A quick cash sale eliminates these ongoing financial obligations and provides immediate liquidity for both spouses to establish new living arrangements.

Many Hampton Roads families going through divorce find that a cash sale provides the fastest path to a fresh start. There are no open houses, no repair requests from buyers, and no risk of deals falling through due to financing issues.
