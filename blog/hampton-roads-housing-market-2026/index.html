Hampton Roads Housing Market 2026

Published December 2, 2025 · Updated March 28, 2026

Hampton Roads Housing Market Overview for 2026

The Hampton Roads real estate market continues to evolve in 2026, presenting unique opportunities and challenges for homeowners looking to sell. With military installations, shipbuilding industries, and a growing tech sector driving demand, the region remains one of the most dynamic housing markets in Virginia.

Whether you own property in Virginia Beach, Norfolk, Chesapeake, or any of the surrounding cities, understanding current market conditions can help you make informed decisions about selling your home.

Home Prices and Market Conditions

Median home prices across Hampton Roads have seen steady growth over the past several years. Virginia Beach leads the region with higher median sale prices, while cities like Portsmouth and Newport News offer more affordable entry points. The overall trend shows a market that favors sellers, though rising interest rates have tempered some buyer enthusiasm.

Inventory levels remain relatively tight across the region, which means well-priced homes continue to attract multiple offers. However, homes that need significant repairs or updating may sit on the market longer as buyers become more selective with their budgets.

What This Means for Sellers

If you are considering selling your home in Hampton Roads, the current market conditions present several paths forward. Traditional listings through a real estate agent can maximize your sale price if your home is in good condition and you have time to wait for the right buyer.

For homeowners who need to sell quickly or whose properties need repairs, a cash sale may be the better option. Cash buyers can close in as little as one to two weeks and purchase homes in any condition, eliminating the need for costly renovations and lengthy negotiations.

Key Factors Affecting the Hampton Roads Market

Several factors continue to shape the local real estate landscape. The military presence at Naval Station Norfolk and Joint Base Langley-Eustis creates consistent housing demand through PCS relocations. The expansion of the Hampton Roads Bridge-Tunnel is also impacting property values in surrounding areas.

Additionally, the growing remote work trend has brought new residents to the region who are attracted to the area's lower cost of living compared to northern Virginia and the Washington DC metro area. This influx of buyers keeps demand strong across most price points.

Tips for Selling in the Current Market

Regardless of which selling method you choose, there are several steps you can take to position yourself for success. Price your home competitively based on recent comparable sales in your neighborhood. Be prepared for inspections and appraisals if going the traditional route. And if time is a factor, explore cash offer options that can provide certainty and speed.

The Hampton Roads market rewards prepared sellers. Whether you list with an agent or accept a cash offer, understanding your local market conditions puts you in the strongest possible position.
