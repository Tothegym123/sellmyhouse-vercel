Selling Without a Realtor in Virginia (FSBO)

Published August 26, 2025 · Updated January 15, 2026

Can You Sell Your House Without a Realtor in Virginia?

Selling your home without a real estate agent, commonly known as For Sale By Owner or FSBO, is perfectly legal in Virginia and can save you thousands of dollars in commission fees. However, going the FSBO route comes with its own set of challenges that every Hampton Roads homeowner should understand before making this decision.

The average real estate commission in Virginia ranges from 5 to 6 percent of the sale price. On a home worth 300,000 dollars, that translates to 15,000 to 18,000 dollars in agent fees. It is easy to see why many homeowners consider handling the sale themselves.

Steps to Sell FSBO in Virginia

Price Your Home Accurately

One of the biggest mistakes FSBO sellers make is mispricing their home. Research recent comparable sales in your Hampton Roads neighborhood to determine a competitive asking price. You can also hire an appraiser for a professional opinion, which typically costs 300 to 500 dollars.

Prepare and Market Your Property

Invest in professional photography and write a compelling listing description. List your home on the MLS through a flat-fee listing service, and promote it on social media and FSBO websites. First impressions matter, so make sure your home is clean, decluttered, and staged for showings.

Handle Showings and Negotiations

Be prepared to schedule and conduct showings yourself, often on short notice. When offers come in, you will need to review them carefully, negotiate terms, and manage counteroffers without professional representation.

Manage the Legal Paperwork

Virginia requires specific disclosures and legal documents to complete a real estate transaction. You will need a purchase agreement, property disclosure form, lead paint disclosure for homes built before 1978, and various closing documents. Consider hiring a real estate attorney to review everything before closing.

Challenges of Selling FSBO

Statistics show that FSBO homes typically sell for less than agent-listed properties. The National Association of Realtors reports that FSBO homes sell for a median of 30,000 to 40,000 dollars less than agent-assisted sales. This is often attributed to less market exposure, less effective pricing strategies, and weaker negotiation outcomes.

Additionally, FSBO sellers invest significant time and energy managing every aspect of the sale. From handling buyer inquiries to coordinating inspections and appraisals, the process can become overwhelming, especially if you are also managing a job, family, or relocation.

An Alternative: Sell to a Cash Buyer

If your primary motivation for selling FSBO is avoiding commissions, consider selling to a cash home buyer instead. Cash buyers charge no commissions or fees, and they handle all the paperwork and closing costs. You get the financial benefit of avoiding agent fees with none of the hassle of marketing and showing your home yourself.
