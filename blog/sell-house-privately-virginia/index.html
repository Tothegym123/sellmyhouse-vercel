Sell Your House Privately in Virginia

Published November 4, 2025 · Updated February 25, 2026

How to Sell Your House Privately in Virginia

Not every homeowner wants to go through the traditional process of listing their home on the MLS, hosting open houses, and having strangers walk through their property. Whether you value your privacy, need to sell discreetly due to personal circumstances, or simply want to avoid the hassle of a public listing, there are several ways to sell your Hampton Roads home privately.

Why Some Homeowners Prefer Private Sales

There are many legitimate reasons homeowners choose to sell privately. You may be going through a divorce and want to keep the sale out of public view. Perhaps you are a public figure or simply a private person who does not want neighbors and strangers knowing your business. Some homeowners are dealing with financial difficulties and prefer not to broadcast that they need to sell.

Others simply want to avoid the disruption of traditional selling. Keeping your home show-ready at all times, accommodating last-minute showing requests, and dealing with curious neighbors can be exhausting, especially for families with children or pets.

Options for Selling Privately

Direct Sale to a Cash Buyer

The most private way to sell your home is directly to a cash buyer. There is no public listing, no yard sign, no open houses, and no parade of strangers through your home. You receive a private offer, negotiate directly, and close on your timeline. The entire transaction can be completed discreetly with minimal public exposure.

Pocket Listing Through an Agent

Some real estate agents offer pocket listings, also known as off-market listings. Your home is marketed only to the agent's network of buyers and other agents rather than being listed on the public MLS. While this limits your exposure and potentially your sale price, it provides a level of privacy that traditional listings do not.

Word of Mouth and Personal Network

You can quietly spread the word among friends, family, and colleagues that your home is available. This approach costs nothing but limits your pool of potential buyers to people within your extended network.

Legal Requirements for Private Sales in Virginia

Even in a private sale, Virginia law requires certain disclosures. You must provide a Residential Property Disclosure Statement to the buyer, and if your home was built before 1978, you are required to provide lead paint disclosures. A real estate attorney can help ensure all legal requirements are met even without an agent involved in the transaction.

Title insurance, a property survey, and a clear title search are still necessary components of any private sale. Working with a reputable title company or closing attorney protects both you and the buyer.

Benefits of a Private Cash Sale

For Hampton Roads homeowners who value discretion, a private cash sale offers the best of both worlds. You maintain your privacy while still receiving a fair offer for your property. There are no commissions, no public listings, and no prolonged exposure of your personal situation. The transaction is handled professionally and confidentially from start to finish.
