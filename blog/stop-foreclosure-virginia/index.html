Stop Foreclosure in Virginia

Published September 23, 2025 · Updated February 5, 2026

How to Stop Foreclosure in Virginia

Falling behind on mortgage payments is a frightening experience, but foreclosure is not inevitable. If you are a homeowner in Hampton Roads facing the possibility of losing your home, there are several options available to help you avoid foreclosure and protect your financial future.

Virginia is a non-judicial foreclosure state, which means lenders can foreclose without going through the court system. This makes the process faster, so it is critical to act quickly once you receive a notice of default or notice of sale.

Understanding the Virginia Foreclosure Timeline

In Virginia, the foreclosure process can move relatively quickly compared to other states. After you miss several payments, your lender will send a notice of default. If you do not cure the default, the lender can schedule a foreclosure sale with as little as 14 days notice after filing the required legal notices.

The entire process from the first missed payment to the foreclosure auction can take as few as 60 to 90 days in Virginia. This compressed timeline makes it essential to explore your options as early as possible.

Options to Avoid Foreclosure

Loan Modification

Contact your lender as soon as you realize you cannot make your payments. Many lenders offer loan modification programs that can reduce your monthly payment, extend your loan term, or temporarily lower your interest rate. The key is to reach out before you are severely delinquent.

Forbearance Agreement

A forbearance agreement allows you to temporarily pause or reduce your mortgage payments while you get back on your feet. This is often a good option if your financial hardship is temporary, such as a job loss or medical emergency.

Short Sale

If you owe more than your home is worth, your lender may agree to a short sale where you sell the property for less than the outstanding mortgage balance. This requires lender approval and can be a lengthy process, but it is generally less damaging to your credit than a foreclosure.

Sell Your Home for Cash

One of the fastest ways to stop foreclosure is to sell your home to a cash buyer before the auction date. A cash sale can close in as little as seven days, allowing you to pay off your mortgage, avoid the foreclosure on your credit report, and potentially walk away with equity in your pocket.

Protecting Your Credit and Future

A foreclosure can remain on your credit report for seven years and make it difficult to purchase another home for several years after that. By taking action early and exploring alternatives like a cash sale, you can minimize the damage to your credit score and keep more options available for your financial future.

If you are behind on payments in Hampton Roads, do not wait until the last minute. Reach out to explore your options and find the best path forward for your situation.
